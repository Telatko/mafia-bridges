Terminology used here

  • River identifies the body of water over which a draw bridge is suspended. Let's call them the west river and the east river. They are separated by Central Island but merge again approaching the Terranova bridge.
  • Ship spawn identifies a position where a ship can be spawned at when a city scene is (re)loaded. There are 6 such positions for each river. Ids w1, w2, w3, w4, w5, w6 represent positions placed in the west river while ids e1, e2, e3, e4, e5, e6 represent positions placed in the east river.
  • Ship respawn identifies a spot a ship can be respawned at after it reaches its previous destination. Ids ws, wn, es, en represent the southern and northern respawn positions for the west and the east ship respectively.
  • Bridge case describes a time window during which the West or the East Marshall bridge is raised.
  • River case is a sequence of one or two bridge cases for either West or the East Marshall bridge during the first seven minutes from the moment a city scene is (re)loaded.
  • City case identifies a combination of ship spawns and respawns that occur during the first seven minutes from the moment a city scene is (re)loaded. For instance the id of w2e3eswn describes a situation in which the west ship was spawned at w2, the east ship was spawned at e3 and later the east ship was respawned at es and the west ship at wn in this chronological order.

How ships and bridges work in Mafia

Ships don't just randomly decide whether they sail the west or the east river. There's exactly one ship assigned to the West Marshall bridge and exactly one ship assigned to the East Marshall bridge and they never change their assignment.

When you load or reload a city scene both the west and the east ships are randomly spawned at one of their six predefined positions. The west ship always sails northward and the east ship always sails southward until they reach a certain point. After that the ship that reached its destination is respawned at its either southern or northern respawn position continuing northwards or southwards respectively.

When a ship approches its draw bridge you can hear its horn provided that you are close enough to the ship. At the same time the red lights start signaling that the bridge is about to be raised. There are two exceptions to this procedure however, both tied to the moment immediately after loading or reloading a city scene. If you can hear the ship horn near Salieri bar immediately after loading then it means the west ship was spawned at w2. In this case the bridge stays down and there are no red lights. If the east ship is spawned at e1 then the bridge will be raised even though the ship actually sails away from the bridge. If the ship is then respawned at en the bridge starts going down after just 10 seconds fully raised. If it's respawned at es however the bridge will stay up until the ship goes from the respawn position all the way down passed the bridge which takes a lot of time.

It's only loading or reloading a city scene that resets ship positions. Getting a quick save doesn't change anything, loading it does.

When ships approach each other in the section between Central Island and Terranova bridge one of them usually yields to allow the other ship to pass first. There are situations however in which none of the ships yields and then they pass right through each other.

While this is never an issue in speedruns please note that all the timing information and bridge up schedules you can find in this resource assume that bridges are never prevented from going up. The movable span of the bridge needs to be free of any traffic before it can be raised and ships are happy to wait until this condition is met.

Audible range of a ship horn

In order to be able to hear its horn you need to be close enough to the ship in question. The further away you are the less powerful the sound is. The furthermost distance from the ship where the sound is still slightly noticeable, if you turn off music and car sounds and the overall audio is very loud, is about 685 meters. In practice it's always less depending on your setup. Technically the limit is likely set to 700 meters but you would have to set the volume insanely high to register the faint sound at that distance.

The horn counts as general sounds so make sure the first bar in the audio settings is set to sufficient volume. If you listen to music during your runs it's better to pause it in times you need to focus. You should experiment yourself to get the best setup suited to you.

Bridge strats

The Running Man Taxi rank

There's exactly 1⁄3 chance that the bridge is up when you get there. Since this is at the beginning of your run it makes sense to reset when it happens. If you don't want to reset you can just reload and hope you are more lucky next time. Every time you reload you lose over 20 seconds though. If you'd rather cross the river via the Giuliano bridge, see this comparison.

Fairplay

In the night part of the mission you have the option to reload at the garage before driving the race car to Lucas Bertone.

If you can hear the ship horn at the beginning of the city segment right after leaving Salieri bar then don't reload at the garage since this signals ship spawn w2 which can only result in river case Z which you don't need to worry about.

If you can't hear the ship horn at the beginning of the city segment then you need to decide whether or not to reload at the garage.

If you reload

If you reload then it's only the ship spawn w1 that is of concern because it leads to river case Q. If you are a fast runner and you don't get any significant time loss on your way there you should be able to cross the bridge before it's too late even if you get Q. To delay the ship a little bit to your favor you can use the inventory trick while starting the engine of the race car. The chance of getting the ship spawn w1 is 1⁄6. If you can see the red lights or hear the ship horn at 1:16 (not accounting for the inventory trick) when approaching the bridge you need to decide whether you want to jump the bridge or not. The bridge is somewhat easily jumpable till about 1:35-1:40 from the moment you reload the game. After that it becomes risky and you might damage the car.

If you don't want to jump the bridge and you are fine with sacrificing about 20 seconds you can head for the tunnel instead. This is a fallback solution in case the bridge is up and you don't want to take the risk jumping it. Another way is to go around completely in which case you only lose about 7 seconds compared to the East Marshall Bridge route but unfortunately that's a guaranteed time loss even if the bridge is down.

To compare the strats described above see this video.

Reloading with extra safe margin

It takes close to 3 seconds to start up the engine of the race car. That is the time during which you can employ the inventory trick without losing any time. There's one more way to gain extra safe margin to get to the bridge in time. Right before Bobby finishes unlocking the garage start spamming the action button to open the door before he does while making sure the angle between Tommy and the garage door prevents unnecessary menus asking which door you want to open. Quicky get out of the cutscene trigger zone and get in the car. Once the engine is running drive towards Bobby to trigger the cutscene and after the cutscene is over reload the checkpoint as usual. After reloading you no longer need to get in the car and you no longer need to start up the engine. This means you can get to the bridge around 8 seconds earlier although it can't be combined with the inventory trick so the actual extra safe margin is about 5-6 seconds. The downside of this strat is that it loses about 3 seconds compared to the usual reload strat. See the strat in action.

Triggering the cutscene with the race car is a bit tricky as you need to get pretty close to Bobby. If you hit him less than gently you get extra time loss as the impact throws him away and the cutscene won't start until he gets up again. Ideally you either want to prevent that or you intentionally hit him hard to aggro him so that you can save time later.

If you don't reload

If you don't reload then the ship spawn w5 or w6 followed by the respawn ws is what you need to worry about. The chance of getting w5ws is 1⁄12, the same applies for w6ws.

  • Ship spawn w5ws leads to river case S. This river case is a bit similar to Q in that you should be able to cross the bridge before it's too late unless you get a significant time loss on your way there. Again, you can use the inventory trick to get extra safe margin. The bridge is somewhat easily jumpable till about 4:08-4:13 from the moment you loaded the city segment. If you don't think you can cross the bridge in time you can again head for the tunnel.
  • Ship spawn w6ws leads to river case R. If you are a fast runner and you don't get any significant time loss on your way to the bridge then you will have to wait 15-20 seconds before it becomes easily jumpable. Obviously if you already had some time loss since loading the city segment the wait time will be shorter. In this case it doesn't make any sense to use the tunnel.
  • It would be nice to know in advance whether you get w5ws or w6ws. Unfortunately you would have to be losing at most 5-10 seconds against this attempt so that at 3:49 from the moment of loading the city segment you would be close enough to the bridge in order to potentially hear the ship horn identifying the river case S. If you don't get the ship horn but you can see the red lights flashing then you know it's the river case R. This information is useless however because if you were fast enough to get the ship horn then you know you can easily jump the bridge in case S and if you got the case R then it's still faster to wait and jump the bridge rather than heading for the tunnel. If you are too slow to get there in time you won't be able to say whether the flashing red lights signal S or R. With the former it would be faster to head for the tunnel and with the latter it would be faster to wait until you can jump the bridge.

Ultimately it depends on your skills whether it makes sense to reload at the garage. I recommend that you experiment with reloading and test your ability to get to the bridge in time and improve your jumping skills. And don't forget to use the inventory trick. If you still find it challenging you can decide not to reload at the garage. This will split the otherwise 1⁄6 chance to screw up to equal halves giving you the 1⁄12 chance that you get the long wait with S or the short wait with R.

The Whore

Going over the East Marshall Bridge saves about 10 seconds over the Giuliano Bridge.

If you decide to take the risk there are two cases of concern:

  • Ship spawn e1 followed by the respawn es is a run killer as it leads to river case C. The chance of that is 1⁄12. In this case you lose about 45 seconds waiting for the bridge to become jumpable. Unfortunately there's no way to predict this case.
  • Ship spawn e3 leads to river case D. The chance of getting this ship spawn is 1⁄6. If you are a fast runner you should be able to easily jump the bridge before it gets too high. You can use the inventory trick to get a bit of extra safe margin however that also freezes the tram approaching Salieri Bar and later it can be in your way. If you are a slower runner or you get a significant time loss on your way to the bridge there's an easy way to identify this case. At 1:04 from the moment of loading the city segment you should be able to hear the ship horn. In this case you can make a U-turn and head for the Giuliano Bridge. You'll lose up to around 15 seconds compared to going to the Giuliano Bridge directly. In order to be able to hear the ship horn you need to be at least around the traffic light intersection right after the tunnel. See the details tab.

Bon Apptit!

Going over the West Marshall bridge can save a few seconds compared to the Terranova bridge. The route is more difficult however. If you decide to go for it the only relevant ship spawn is w1 which leads to river case Q. The chance of that is 1⁄6 and unless you lose more than 15 seconds on your way there you should not have any issues jumping over the ascending bridge.

You Lucky Bastard!

In this mission we care about the East Marshall bridge in the Rainbow garden segment and the subsequent Escape part. Taking the draw bridge in the Rainbow garden part can save between 15 and 20 seconds compared to the Giuliano bridge route but the bridge can be up by the time you get there. Fortunately we have decent options to avoid that. You can reload the Rainbow garden and Escape parts if the odds aren't in your favor.

Fireworks

In the Fireworks part pay attention what happens at around 1:04. At that time you should be on top of the Giuliano bridge unless you lose time on your way there.

  • If you can hear a ship horn at 1:04 then you got the ship spawn e3 which leads to either river case D or DH. The probability of getting this ship spawn is 1⁄6 and then the probability of getting D vs DH is 1⁄2 for each. The former isn't an issue since the bridge case D is completed before you even place the bomb. However the latter is a problem as the bridge case H can take place just when you want to cross the East Marshall bridge on your way to Rainbow garden. If you are very fast with no time loss you can still jump the bridge. If for whatever reason you're losing more than 1 minute and 20 seconds you are also good to go as the bridge should be going down by the time you get there. Those are extreme cases however and in most situations it's probably a good idea to reload after the Fireworks part when you get the ship horn at 1:04. In order to be able to hear the horn you need to be close enough to the ship, see the details tab.
  • If there's a ship on your left while you are on top of the Giuliano bridge then you got the ship spawn e5 which leads to river case G/ GL. The chance of getting this ship spawn is 1⁄6. If you get it I recommend you reload after you finish the Fireworks part unless you lost more than 30 seconds in the Fireworks part. Otherwise you're going to have to deal with the bridge case G and probably wait around 30 seconds until the bridge becomes jumpable.
  • If there's a ship on your right while you are on top of the Giuliano bridge then you got the ship spawn e4 which leads to river case E/ EJ. The bridge case E is not an issue since it is over before you get to the bridge. J could be a problem if you lose more than 25 seconds from the moment of loading the Fireworks part and the time you cross the bridge on your way back to Salieri bar in the Escape part. The probability of getting E vs EJ is 1⁄2 for each. If you are a slower runner or you get a significant time loss during the Fireworks segment then you might consider reloading after you finish the segment. Or you can decide to reload later after you finish the Rainbow Garden part.
  • If you don't get the ship horn at 1:04 and there was no ship in sight from the top of the Giuliano bridge then you don't need to worry about the bridge in the Rainbow garden and Escape segments.

Before reaching the top of the Giuliano bridge I recommend switching to the camera that is the furthest away from your car. With this camera it's much easier to check whether there are any ships in the vicinity of the bridge. First stick to the right side of the road. You should be able to see the river on the right side without using side cameras or looking around using the mouse. If you can see a ship there then you know you got the ship spawn e4. Otherwise move closer to the left side of the road and use the mouse to look left. If you can see a ship there you know you got the ship spawn e5. If the ship isn't there the only remaining thing to do is to check whether you get a ship horn. If you can hear it you know you got the ship spawn e3. Otherwise you didn't get either of e3, e4 and e5 and therefore the draw bridge is guaranteed to be down during the Rainbow Garden and Escape parts assuming you reload neither. To get an idea what to look for you can see this video where I show all 3 cases in a single video. Of course in the game you can only get at most 1 of them.

If checking both ships is too much for you then you can try skipping the right side and focus just on the left side. Even if there was a ship on the right side, indicating you got the ship spawn e4, it still wouldn't be a problem as long as you don't get any significant time loss. Another approach is to detect the ship spawn e4 by means of hearing the ship horn in Downtown after your arrival to Oakwood. You have to wait for the guard to finish his smoke break anyway. If at 2:05 from the moment of loading the city scene you can't hear any ship horn you know you didn't get this ship spawn. This strat introduces additional problems to worry about though. It's more driving so more opportunity to flip the car. You also lose the option to easily get rid of cops who often follow you to Oakwood.

In order to be able to hear ship horns further away from the source I recommend adjusting audio levels in the settings so that music and car sounds are only half of the volume you set for general sounds. This setup is used in videos mentioned in the previous two paragraphs.

Rainbow garden

If, for whatever reason, you decided to reload at the beginning of this segment then

  • If you get the ship spawn e1 followed by the respawn es then you'll unfortunately get the river case C and you'd have to wait 1 minute and 10 seconds for the bridge to become jumpable again. The chance of that is 1⁄12. Unfortunately there's no way to detect this case before you get to the bridge. If you get the respawn en then you get the river case AM/ AN which is fine. In this case the bridge finishes its descent just before you get there.
  • If you get the ship spawn e2 then you get the river case B/ BF and you might have to wait a few seconds till the bridge is jumpable. The probability of this case is 1⁄6.
  • If you get the ship spawn e3 then you get the river case D/ DH. In this case the bridge should be down when you get there on your way to Rainbow garden (unless you got a significant time loss) but unfortunately it's going to be up on your way back to Salieri bar. Luckily you can detect this case and reload once you get to Rainbow garden. You should be able to hear the ship horn and the bells indicating the bridge is about to be raised.

If you are unfortunate to get the ship spawn e1 followed by the respawn es leading to the river case C but you don't want to reset and prefer to just finish the segment then the most reasonable way to do that is to turn around and head for the Giuliano bridge. In fact if you reload after the Fireworks part and then the bridge is completely up by the time you get there you should U-turn immediately but still pay attention whether the bridge starts going down at 0:58. The chance is 2⁄3 that it does. If it does then go back again and jump the bridge since this signals that you got the ship spawn e2. If it doesn't however then unfortunately you need to stick with the original plan and go around via the Giuliano bridge. It's a long route but it's still about 13 seconds faster than just waiting.

There is one more alternative to the Giuliano bridge and that is to reload the segment if by 0:59 the bridge still isn't going down. If the bridge is down on your second try then this approach is about 1 second faster than going around but of course the chance is 1⁄12 that you get another ship spawn e1 followed by the respawn es and further 1⁄6 chance that you get the ship spawn e2 turning the 1 second time save into time loss, a severe one in case of the former. Also, if you instead decide to go around (or wait) then the bridge is guaranteed to be down in the Escape part, unless you reload there of course.

Escape

If you have to reload the escape part then these are the cases to worry about:

  • Ship spawn e1 with either the es or en respawn. The former leads to river case C and the latter leads to river case AM/ AN. Each of the two cases happens with the probability of 1⁄12, so 1⁄6 in total.
  • Ship spawn e2 which leads to river case B/ BF. The chance of this ship spawn is 1⁄6.

The odds are 1⁄3 that bridge will be going up when you get there. The bridge starts going up immediately after you reload the scene. Unfortunately you won't hear the ship horn.

Here are some of the strats you may choose:

  • Reload the segment if you can see the red lights signaling that the bridge is going up. You are able to see the red lights about 15 seconds after reloading the segment. Since the chance of getting bridge up is 1⁄3 this strat loses about 5 seconds per run on average.
  • Jump the bridge if it's going up. If you don't lose any time on your way to the bridge there's an easy way to jump the bridge. Go full speed the entire time until you approach the bridge. Then slow down to about 40 mph, lock your gearbox to the first gear and jump the bridge with the maximum speed the first gear allows you to go. This works consistently because the bridge is still only halfway up and the slope is too mild to significantly influence the velocity of your car in any unpredictable way. The speed is just right to make it to the other side with a significant safe margin yet not high enough to flip your car before landing. The only thing to worry about is not losing time on your way to the bridge and not getting pushed by the gangster car while jumping the bridge. You don't have the spare time to crash or get slowed down by Sergio's people. You also can't efford to lose any of your tires but you can use the front bumper camera to make sure you don't.
  • Use the front bumper camera to skip the need to start the egine and start driving away immediately after skipping the cutscene where Paulie attempts to assassinate Sergio. This saves nearly four seconds of time and you won't be harassed by Sergio's people because you're long gone before they enter their car. Jump the bridge in the same way except your speed should be between 20 and 30 mph before entering the movable span of the bridge. The only problem with this strat is that Paulie can kick the bucket if you leave him behind. The chance of that seems to be around 20% unless you push him and then the gangster car often drives him over. However with the front bumper camera you don't have to worry about that. Fortunately Paulie is unlikely to die past about 10 seconds after reloading the segment since that's when the gangster car leaves the parking lot. Simply reload the segment if you get unlucky.

If you get the bridge up but you don't feel like reloading or jumping the bridge you can also consider going for the longer route via the Giuliano bridge. This option loses a lot of time however.

  • If you can see the red lights then turn right just before the bridge, go north alongside the river and get to the Giuliano bridge using the shortcut behind Bertone's.
  • Or you can first check which ship spawn you got. If you can see a ship on the right after getting on the bridge then you know you got the e2 spawn in which case the bridge won't come down any time soon and you should make a U-turn and fallback to the Giuliano route.
  • If you can't see the ship then you got the e1 spawn. In this case it's 1⁄2 chance that the bridge starts going down after just 10 seconds from the moment it's fully raised. So in this case you can simply wait to see what happens. If you can hear a ship horn 6 seconds after the bridge is fully raised than don't waste any more time and fallback to the Giuliano route. If you don't get the ship horn however than the bridge will start going down only 4 seconds after that.
  • Or you can head for the Giuliano bridge immediately after you reload the segment. This option is always faster than going there later.

Election Campaign - Bertone Mission

In this Bertone mission it is faster to go to Works Quarter via the West Marshall bridge rather than the tunnel.

You should always reload after the dialog with Lucas because in that case you don't need to worry about the bridge too much. The only potential case of concern is the ship spawn w1 which leads to river case Q. However you would have to be losing more than half a minute not to be able to at least jump the bridge. It's more likely that you make it to the other side before the bridge even starts going up. At the same time the bridge finishes its descent before you get back to cross the river on your way to Lucas and that is even if you are a fast runner.

If you don't reload for whatever reason these are the cases to worry about:

  • Ship spawn w5 followed by the respawn ws leads to river case S. The chance of getting this case is 1⁄12. The bridge will be down on your way to Works Quarter but not on your way back to Lucas. If you don't get any significant time loss you should still be able to jump the bridge though.
  • Ship spawn w6 followed by the respawn ws leads to river case R. The chance of getting this case is 1⁄12. Again the bridge will be down on your way to Works Quarter but not on your way back to Lucas. However it should be about get down soon. Even if you are a fast runner and you don't get any significant time loss you should be good to go after just a few seconds of waiting.

Just For Relaxation

It is faster to use the West Marshall bridge when taking the truck to the warehouse. There is only one case you need to worry about and that's the ship spawn w1 because it leads to river case Q. The odds of getting this case is 1⁄6. If the bridge is up you won't be able to jump it until around 2:35 from the moment you loaded the segment. The time loss is about 25 seconds in case you took the safer route to Paulie and Sam or 35 seconds if you took the faster route. Add about 7 seconds to both cases if you used the inventory trick to gain advantage against the port guards. Subtract any time you lost fighting them in case there was a need for it.

There's also the option to head for the tunnel instead. It can be a few seconds faster compared to waiting for the bridge to become jumpable but only if you took the faster route to Paulie and Sam or you used the inventory trick to delay the guards and at the same time you switch to this route immeditaly after seeing the red lights and you don't lose any significant amount of time anywhere.
